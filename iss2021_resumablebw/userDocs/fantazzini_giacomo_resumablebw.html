Lab ISS | the project resumableBoundaryWalker

Introduction

This case-study starts to deal with the design and development of proactive/reactive software systems which work under user-control.

Requirements

Design and build a software system (named from now on 'the application') that leads the robot described in VirtualRobot2021.html to walk along the boundary of a empty, rectangular room under user control.
More specifically, the user story can be summarized as follows:
the robot is initially located at the HOME position, as shown in the picture on the rigth
the application presents to the user a consoleGui similar to that shown in the picture on the rigth
when the user hits the button RESUME the robot starts or continue to walk along the boundary, while updating a robot-moves history;
when the user hits the button STOP the robot stop its journey, waiting for another RESUME ;
when the robot reachs its HOME again, the application shows the robot-moves history on the standard output device.

Requirement analysis

Those terms already extensively described in VirtualRobot2021 and BoundaryWalk will not be repeated.

A consoleGui is any graphical application or part of an application able to receive user input.

To STOP means to temporarily stop moving while being capable of resuming the interrupted movement and sequence of movements.

To RESUME means to start a predetermined set of movements or to resume it after a STOP.

The robot-moves history is what enables the STOP-RESUME mechanism. It must be presentable in a human-readable format.

Problem analysis

As stated in VirtualRobot2021, the virtual robot can communicate over port 8090 via HTTP POST or over port 8091 via WebSocket. To ensure more flexibility and to move toward a more asynchronous model, we recommend the usage of the WebSocket protocol.

The problem strongly suggests a distributed architecture with two software macro-components: the virtual robot given by the customer and our application (the resumable boundary walker). The overall logical system architecture is thus the one already discussed in BoundaryWalk.

The final structure, behaviour and interaction of the software components will be up to the designer. However, to discuss the particular problem posed by the STOP-RESUME mechanism, we may start by taking for granted the architecture with proactive and reactive behaviour discussed in VirtualRobotClients and work on it.

We need to enhance this architecture by making it capable of pausing and resuming its behaviour at will. This capability is for sure not part of the core business logic and should be kept well distinct from it. It may be thought either as a service, which is part of the infrastructure, or as a meta business logic.

For a first prototype, we propose to extend the RobotInputController by making it capable of suspending the feedback loop of messages that the business logic in RobotBoundaryLogic relies on to keep firing new commands to the robot.

The new MyRobotInputController, upon receiving a robotcmd=STOP message, would stop forwarding endmove messages to the RobotBoundaryLogic and, upon receiving a robotcmd=RESUME message, would resume its behaviour. While being stopped, it needs to store the next endmove message received from the robot in order to pass it over to the business logic later, while resuming the execution. Every other message received during this phase will be dropped. It is also assumed that while being paused the infrastructure would receive no more than one endmove message from the robot.

The assumptions just made are quite cumbersome and could pose a problem if any generalization will ever be needed. However, they seem pretty reasonable given the specific nature of the RobotBoundaryLogic business logic. It is also worth noting that the RobotInputController from the base architecture is anyway already very dependant on the specific business logic implemented in RobotBoundaryLogic, even without these assumptions.

As of the nature of the ConsoleGui, we propose to strip it of any business or infrastructure logic and make it just fire robotcmd messages to the infrastructure.

It is important to note that the proposed solution does not support mid-action pauses and it always stops the execution after the current action has been fully performed.
